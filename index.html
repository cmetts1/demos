Current speech synthesis models strive to generate human-like speech. Emotional speech synthesis is paramount in text-to-speech (TTS) research within this framework. In this study, we employ Consistency Models (CMs) as the foundation and introduce a series of enhancements to tackle the pivotal challenges in emotional speech synthesis. Our primary objective is to accurately model and control the emotional intensity in the generated speech. To guarantee that variations in emotional intensities affect each phoneme, we leverage Global Style Tokens (GST) to learn emotion vector embeddings and derive utterance-level embeddings. Furthermore, we incorporate Conditional Layer Normalization (CLN) and Conditional Cross-Attention (CCA) blocks to adjust feature weights based on the specified emotion. Drawing inspiration from continuous-time diffusion models, we propose a novel architecture grounded in Consistency Models. This architecture not only enhances the quality of synthesized speech but also addresses the inefficiency of multi-step sampling in diffusion models, thereby achieving high-quality speech synthesis with reduced steps. The efficacy of our model is substantiated through ablation studies, highlighting the significance of each component. Both automatic evaluations and human assessments demonstrate substantial improvements in Mean Opinion Scores (MOS) and emotion recognition accuracy for synthesized speech.
